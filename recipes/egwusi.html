Egwusi Soup

Description

Egwusi soup is a recipe indigenous to the Igbo people of Biafra. This recipe is rich in protein, vitamins and essential minerals; adding to the cassava-extracted food - popularly known as Garri - with which it is eaten. This is more of a one-armed couple walking on an isle to the peak of a soul-healing adventure.

What more could you ask for than the privilege to be served a plate of this delicious recipe, to skyrocket your overall wellness to an amazing height?

Ingredients

  • Melon
  • Palm oil
  • Stock fish
  • Dried fish
  • Meat
  • Ugba
  • Ogiri
  • Pepper
  • Crayfish
  • Pumpkin leaf
  • Mgbaakparata
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Ginger

Steps

  1. Wash your meat and fishes thoroughly and:
    • Turn a kilogram of water into the pot
    • Boil the meat and fishes for thirty minutes
    • Add 10g of onions
    • Add a teaspoon of salt
    • Wait for another ten minutes and add adequate palm oil.
  2. Add your grounded melon into the pot.
  3. After seven minutes of boiling, add ugba and ogiri.
  4. Grind and add half bulb of ginger and moderate garlic.
  5. Stir the soup thoroughly and close the pot.
  6. Add crayfish, pumpkin leaf, bitter leaf, and salt.
  7. Wait for another five minutes and stir the soup.
